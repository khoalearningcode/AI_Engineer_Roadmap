Training Principles

How to Use This Roadmap

This is not a checklist to read. It is a training system. Every skill should become a project, every project should produce evidence, and every evidence should improve your interview story.

01

Do not collect keywords. Build proof.

A skill only counts when it becomes something visible: a GitHub repo, benchmark table, architecture diagram, failure analysis, technical write-up, or deployable demo.

02

Follow the loop: Understand → Build → Benchmark → Write → Explain.

Learn the concept, build a working version, measure it, write down the trade-offs, then explain it clearly in 2–5 minutes as if you were in an interview.

03

Main spine first, expansion later.

The core path is RAG → Agents → Serving → GPU Inference → AI Factory. Generative models, visual intelligence, and physical AI are strategic differentiators, not distractions from the main path.

04

Measure like an engineer.

For RAG, measure recall@k, MRR, faithfulness, latency, and failure cases. For inference, measure TTFT, TPOT, tokens/sec, concurrency, GPU memory, and cost per task.

05

Study trends, but do not chase hype blindly.

Track NVIDIA, vLLM, Triton, TensorRT-LLM, Dynamo, NIM, NeMo, agentic AI, multimodal models, diffusion, synthetic data, and physical AI. Then ask: “What can I build from this?”

06

Your goal is not to look busy. Your goal is to become hard to replace.

Become the engineer who can connect product, agents, retrieval, inference, GPU deployment, evaluation, multimodal systems, and long-term AI infrastructure direction.

Operating Rule

70% Build · 20% Study Trends · 10% Polish Portfolio

Spend most of your time building and measuring. Use trend watching to choose better projects, not to replace practice. Polish only after there is real technical evidence to show.
